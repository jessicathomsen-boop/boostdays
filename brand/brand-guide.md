# Strategic foundation

Boost Days 2026 is built on two strategic pillars. Every brand decision, communication, and design choice should trace back to one or both of these.

> \[!TIP] > **Pillar 1: Be BOLD in experimentation**

> \[!TIP] > **Pillar 2: Everyone needs to up skills in the AI Era**

## How we measure ROI on Boost Days

Leadership will ask "was it worth two days of lost productivity?"

We answer with evidence across three dimensions:

| Dimension                | What we measure                                                                                                                           | How we capture it                                                                                     |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| \*\*Skills built \*\*        | % of people who participate in Boost days, Peakon people feeling time to learn less stress because learning is schedule into the workday, | Pulse post-event survey: "What did you learn that you hadn't done before?"                            |
| **Cross- collaboration** | % of teams of people working together                                                                                                     | Pulse post-event survey: Did you collaboration with anyone?                                           |
| **Bold experimentation** | Hack day could be a mini hackathon.                                                                                                       | 5-6 people who make a plan to develop a new feature.                                                  |
| **Ideas that survived**  | % of Boost projects that make it to production, Roadmap, etc.                                                                             | The boost commit will track this? - This is a good place to have an agent tracking the life of ideas. |
| **Engagement & energy**  | 1. Participation rate for non-tech people. PM,QA,UX, Leadership <br />1. Overall participation rate in Products                           | Pulse survey (3 questions max): Quarterly                                                             |

# Brand identity

## Core mission

> \[!NOTE]
> Be bold. Build together. Become AI native. Boost Days gives every person — IC2 to leadership — two days, a crew, and the runway to launch. The skills we build here are the fuel that gets us there.

## The narrative

Boost Days borrows from the optimism of the 1960s space program — not the billionaire space race. Back then, going to the moon was a **shared human project**. It took thousands of people with different skills working together.

They embraced a radical, infectious optimism. It wasn't just about building rockets; it was about the belief that through pure collaboration and human ingenuity, we could navigate the vast unknown and achieve the impossible.

In times that feel dark, that kind of shared exploration isn't just productive—it’s a much-needed breath of fresh air. It reminds us of what we can build together. At e-conomic we are in the AI era and we all are embracing the unknown. That's the spirit we channel.

The rocket metaphor works because it maps directly to our event:

* **The rocket** = your idea, your project
* **The booster** = the skills you build and the crew you assemble
* **The crew =** the people who work together
* **The moon** = the near-term stretch goal — ambitious but visible
* **Mars** = the longer bet that requires real collaboration and new capabilities
* \*\*The open frontier \*\*— "we don't know what we'll find yet"

People self-select their level of ambition. Picking the moon is not less valuable than aiming for Mars. What matters is that you needed a boost to get there — a new skill, a new partnership, a bold experiment.

## Visual identity

The aesthetic is **retro-space** — 1960s mission patches, vintage NASA poster art, flat illustration with bold geometric shapes. It's confident and warm, not slick and corporate.

### Color palette

All colors are pulled from our existing brand system. No off-brand colors.

| Role       | Hex code                | When to use                                                                                   |
| ---------- | ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary    | `#29283E` Midnight Blue | Primary dark background. Deep space. Poster backgrounds, banner fills, slide decks.           |
| Primary    | `#E89C2E` Sunset Orange | Brand accent. Rocket fire. Headlines on dark backgrounds, mission patch borders, CTA buttons. |
| Background | `#FFF5E5` Pale Orange   | Warm cream background. Vintage paper feel. Use instead of white for event-specific materials. |
| Accent     | `#733700` Yellow-900    | Deep warm accent. Text on cream backgrounds. Subtle contrast moments.                         |
| Accent     | `#4573D2` Blue-500      | Cool contrast accent. Planet illustrations, badge highlights, links.                          |

### Typography

* **Display / headlines:** Standard brand sans-serif at heavy weight Opens Sans. For poster moments, consider a chunky geometric display face (Tusker Grotesk, Mango Grotesque, or similar) — but only for big moments, never body copy.
* **Body / communications:** Standard brand body font. All Slack messages, emails, agendas, and documentation use this.

# Tone of voice

## Three keywords

> \[!NOTE] > **Bold**
> We speak with confidence. Ideas are worth backing. "We're going to build this" — not "maybe we could try..."

> \[!NOTE] > **Warm**
> We are kind. We listen. We collaborate and we aren't living on an island, We are whimsical, witty and like to have fun

> \[!NOTE] > **Clear**
> We speak clearly. No jargon walls. A good idea should be explainable to anyone in two minutes.

## Vibe check

> \[!TIP] > **It feels like...**
> Finding your crew for a mission
> 1960s space program optimism
> Whiteboards and late-night demos
> "Wait, you built that in two days?"
> A team-up episode
> An intern teaching a director something new

> \[!CAUTION] > **It doesn't feel like...**
> A corporate hackathon with judges
> Innovation theater for leadership
> Mandatory fun with icebreakers
> A conveyor belt of presentations
> A competition with winners and losers
> "Move fast and break things"
> Something only devs can do

# Taglines

## Primary tagline

> \[!NOTE] > **"What could you build with the right boost?"**
> Use on: banners, slide decks, swag, event landing pages. Works for both pillars — bold experimentation and up skilling.

## Rally cry

> \[!NOTE] > **"Plan your mission. Find your crew. Launch."**
> Use for: kickoff talks, posters, opening slides. The three-beat rhythm drives action.

## Values alignment

> \[!NOTE] > **"Nobody reaches the the unknown alone."**
> Use when: reinforcing cross-level collaboration. Ties directly to "We Are Superheroes" and "We Are Kind." Perfect for closing remarks and retrospectives.

## Up skilling-specific

> \[!NOTE] > **"Every mission builds a new skill."**
> Use when: communicating the ROI angle to leadership, or encouraging people who feel they "don't have the skills" to participate. The boost IS the up skilling.

# Copy guidelines

## Language we use

> \[!TIP] > **Do say**
> "Pick your mission"
> "Find your crew"
> "What's your boost?" (what skill are you building?)
> "Ship a demo"
> "Earn your patch"
> "Launch day"
> "From intern to director"
> "Bold experiment"
> "New territory"

> \[!CAUTION] > **Don't say**
> "Disrupt" / "innovate" (overused, empty)
> "Hack" (it's boost, not hack)
> "Winners and losers"
> "Mandatory participation"
> "Quick wins"
> "Align on deliverables"
> "Senior engineers only"
> "Innovation lab"

| **We Make an Effort**       | Two days of focused effort. Show up, commit, build something you're proud of.                                            | "Two days. Full effort. Ship something that matters."                       |
